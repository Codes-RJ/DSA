# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be respectful and inclusive**: Treat all individuals with respect and consideration
- **Be collaborative**: Work together to create a welcoming environment
- **Be helpful**: Provide assistance and support to others
- **Be constructive**: Focus on what is best for the community
- **Be open**: Welcome newcomers and help them learn

### Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, sexual harassment, or discrimination
- **Disrespectful conduct**: Insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks
- **Spam**: Unwanted content, repetitive posts, or off-topic discussions
- **Privacy violations**: Publishing private information without permission
- **Destructive behavior**: Intentionally causing harm or disruption

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it through:

1. **GitHub Issues**: For public issues and discussions
2. **Private Contact**: For sensitive matters, contact the repository maintainers

### What to Include

- Description of the incident
- Individuals involved (if applicable)
- Date and time of the incident
- Any relevant screenshots or evidence

## Enforcement

### Consequences

Unacceptable behavior may result in:

- **Warning**: Formal notice to stop inappropriate behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Limited access to community resources
- **Permanent Ban**: Complete removal from community spaces

### Appeal Process

If you believe a consequence is unfair, you may:

1. Submit a written appeal to the repository maintainers
2. Provide additional context or evidence
3. Request a review of the decision

## Community Guidelines

### Communication

- **Be clear and concise**: Use clear language and avoid jargon
- **Be patient**: Understand that everyone has different experience levels
- **Be supportive**: Encourage others and celebrate their progress
- **Be professional**: Maintain professional conduct in all interactions

### Collaboration

- **Share knowledge**: Help others learn and grow
- **Give credit**: Acknowledge contributions from others
- **Be open-minded**: Consider different perspectives and approaches
- **Be accountable**: Take responsibility for your actions

## Learning Environment

### Educational Focus

This repository is dedicated to learning Data Structures and Algorithms. We strive to:

- **Create a safe space**: Where everyone feels comfortable asking questions
- **Encourage growth**: Support learners at all skill levels
- **Foster curiosity**: Promote exploration and discovery
- **Build confidence**: Help users develop their skills

### Mentorship

- **Experienced members**: Help guide newcomers
- **Knowledge sharing**: Share insights and best practices
- **Constructive feedback**: Provide helpful and encouraging feedback
- **Lead by example**: Demonstrate positive behavior

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:

- All community spaces (GitHub, discussions, issues, pull requests)
- Both online and offline community interactions
- All community members and contributors

## Contact

For questions about this code of conduct or to report issues, contact:

- **Repository Maintainers**: Through GitHub Issues or private communication
- **Community Managers**: For mediation and support

## Acknowledgment

By participating in this community, you acknowledge that you have read, understood, and agree to abide by this code of conduct.
