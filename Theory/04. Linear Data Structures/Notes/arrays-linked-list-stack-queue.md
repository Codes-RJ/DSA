# Arrays, Linked Lists, Stack, and Queue

## Arrays

- random access in constant time
- insertion or deletion in the middle is costly
- ideal for indexing and traversal problems

## Linked Lists

- nodes connected through pointers or references
- insertion and deletion can be efficient after reaching the position
- random access is slow

## Stack

- operations: push, pop, top
- model: last in, first out
- uses: expression parsing, undo, recursion simulation

## Queue

- operations: enqueue, dequeue, front
- model: first in, first out
- uses: BFS, task scheduling, buffering

## Comparison

- arrays are simple and cache-friendly
- linked lists are flexible in structural updates
- stacks and queues model restricted forms of access
